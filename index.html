Why

Definition: Heuristic refers to experience-based techniques for problem solving, learning, and discovery.

Guidelines to facilitate UX/UI/Visual Design discussions and reviews -- based on goals, not opinions. Non-Designers: Want a healthy, non-opinion-driven conversation with designers. Trying to avoid opinions being seen as rulings, having a lack of direction on what feedback to give and when!

Findability

Findability precedes usability. In the alphabet and on the Web. You can’t use what you can’t find.

Good design is a lot like clear thinking made visual.
— Edward Tufte

Clear

  • Is it easy to understand
  • Path to task completion obvious
  • Simple and succinct
  • Would a user find it easy to describe?
  • Includes messaging/text, not just UI/Visual/UX design

Controllable

Individuals use a control heuristic that includes perceptions of intentionality and connection. Judgments of intentionality are based on foreseeability, ability to produce the effect, and valence of the outcome.

Life's a Dance You Learn As You Go!
— John Montgomery

Learnable

  • Easy to understand after first interaction.
  • Action is repeatable

Consistency

Consistency is one of the most powerful usability principles: when things always behave the same, users don’t have to worry about what will happen. Instead, they know what will happen based on earlier experience.

Credibility is like virginity. Once you lose it, you can never get it back.

Credible

  • Worthy of confidence, reliable

Communicative

Browser windows act as a portal between our input and output devices. How they display websites to users and how those users respond indicate exactly how communicative our work really is.

The only real valuable thing is intuition.
— Albert Einstein

Valuable

  • Of great use, service and importance

Delight

By adding delightful personal touches to your designs, you stand out from the crowd and give visitors something to talk about and share with friends and colleagues.
